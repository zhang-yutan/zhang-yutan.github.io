About

On this page, I share some of my favourite performances of classical music, ballet, opera, and figure skating. All included performances are listed in an essentially random order, and the list will grow with time.

For a complete catalogue of performance recordings, visit unitel.de.

Classical Music (Back to top)

Here I organize by composer-composition in a non-specific order. The majority of the repertoire contains specific versions I enjoy.
Video recordings by Herbert von Karajan, Karl Böhm, Leonard Bernstein and others of their time, were filmed with great visual aesthetic, are more "music movies" than recordings. They will be marked, and are strongly recommended. So are recorded rehearsals with these conductors.

Some fantastic educational resourses for classical music include Young People's Concerts, Omnibus TV programs, The Unanswered Question: Six Talks at Harvard presented by Leonard Bernstein. These are arguably the best broad introduction to classical music, especially orchestral music out there. And Bernstein is one of the greatest music educator of his time. TV script are avaiable from Bernstein's website. Also worth visiting is Herbert von Karajan's website.

Ballet (Back to top)

Here I organize by ballet repertoire in a non-specific order. The majority are complete performances, with a few gala recordings and music selections.
Some recordings were made in the style of "ballet movie". They will be marked, and are strongly recommended.

Some resourses include the 1967 documentary Fundamentals of Classical Dance by the Bolshoi Ballet Academy. The George Balanchine Foundation, The Rudolf Nureyev Foundation, and The Marius Petipa Society provide in-depth analysis and discussion of the artistic styles of these great choreographers.
